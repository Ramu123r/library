1. Student Relation (SQL operations)
A) Student (regno: string, name: string, class: string, bdate: date, marks1:int, marks2:int, marks3:int) i. Enter at least five tuples for the relation. ii. Demonstrate usage of clauses: WHERE, ORDER BY, GROUP BY, HAVING. iii. Demonstrate aggregate functions: SUM, AVG, COUNT, LIKE, BETWEEN, MAX, MIN. iv. Demonstrate ROLLBACK and COMMIT for transactions on this relation.
1.B Employee & Department Relation (SQL / DDL & DML)
B) Employee (empid:int, ename:string, age:int, salary:int, deptno:int) Department (deptno:int, dname:string, manager_id:int) i. Create tables with appropriate PRIMARY KEY and FOREIGN KEY constraints. ii. Insert at least 5 tuples into each table. iii. Query empid & ename whose salary BETWEEN 10000 AND 50000. iv. List ename & salary for employees working in CS department. v. Display ename & deptname for all managers.
2. OrderDatabase Queries (SALESMAN / CUSTOMER / ORDERS)
Schema: SALESMAN (Salesman_id, Name, City, Commission) CUSTOMER (Customer_id, Cust_Name, City, Grade, Salesman_id) ORDERS (Ord_No, Purchase_Amt, Ord_Date, Customer_id, Salesman_id) Write SQL queries to: i. Count customers with grades above Bangalore's average. ii. Find name & ids of salesmen who have more than one customer. iii. List all salesmen and indicate those who have or don’t have customers in their cities (use UNION). iv. Create a view to find salesman who has the customer with the highest order of a day. v. Demonstrate DELETE by removing salesman with id=1000 and cascade delete his orders.
3. Insurances Database (PERSON / CAR / ACCIDENT / OWNS / PARTICIPATED)
Schema: PERSON (driver_id, name, address) CAR (Regno, model, year) ACCIDENT (report_number, date, location) OWNS (driver_id, Regno) PARTICIPATED (driver_id, Regno, report_number, damage_amount) i. Create tables with primary & foreign keys. ii. Insert at least five tuples for each relation. iii.a Update damage_amount for a car with specific Regno in accident report_number=12 to 25000. iii.b Insert a new accident record. iv. Find total people who owned cars involved in accidents in 2002. v. Find total accidents involving cars of a specific model.
4. Book Dealer Database (AUTHOR / PUBLISHER / CATALOG / CATEGORY / ORDER-DETAILS)
Tables: AUTHOR(author-id, name, city, country) PUBLISHER(publisher-id, name, city, country) CATALOG(book-id, title, author-id, publisher-id, category-id, year, price) CATEGORY(category-id, description) ORDER_DETAILS(order-no, book-id, quantity) i. Create tables with PKs and FKs. ii. Insert at least five tuples each. iii. Find authors who have >=2 books in catalog and whose book prices are greater than average price. iv. Find the author of the book with maximum sales. v. Update: increase price of books published by a specific publisher by 10%.
5. Student Enrollment & Book Adoption Database
Schema: STUDENT(regno, name, major, bdate) COURSE(course_id, cname, dept) ENROLL(regno, course_id, sem, marks) TEXT(book_ISBN, book_title, publisher, author) BOOK_ADOPTION(course_id, sem, book_ISBN) i. Create tables with PKs & FKs. ii. Insert at least five tuples each. iii. Add a textbook and adopt it for some department. iv. Produce list of textbooks (Course#, Book-ISBN, Book-title) alphabetically for CS courses that use more than two books. v. List departments that have adopted books published by a specific publisher.
6. Library Management Database
Schema: BOOK(Book_id, Title, Publisher_Name, Pub_Year) BOOK_AUTHORS(Book_id, Author_Name) PUBLISHER(Name, Address, Phone) BOOK_COPIES(Book_id, Branch_id, No_of_Copies) BOOK_LENDING(Book_id, Branch_id, Card_No, Date_Out, Due_Date) LIBRARY_BRANCH(Branch_id, Branch_Name, Address) Write SQL queries to: i. Retrieve details of all books – id, title, publisher, authors, number of copies per branch. ii. Get borrowers who borrowed more than 3 books between Jan 2017 and Jun 2017. iii. Delete a book from BOOK and update other tables accordingly (cascade). iv. Create a view of all books and their available copy counts.
7. Company Database (EMPLOYEE / DEPARTMENT / DLOCATION / PROJECT / WORKS_ON)
Schema: EMPLOYEE(SSN, Name, Address, Sex, Salary, SuperSSN, DNo) DEPARTMENT(DNo, DName, MgrSSN, MgrStartDate) DLOCATION(DNo, DLoc) PROJECT(PNo, PName, PLocation, DNo) WORKS_ON(SSN, PNo, Hours) Write SQL queries to: i. List all project numbers for projects that involve an employee with last name 'Scott', either as worker or as manager of the controlling department. ii. Show resulting salaries if every employee working on the 'IoT' project receives a 10% raise. iii. Compute sum, max, min, avg of salaries for 'Accounts' department. iv. Create a view (dept name, dept location) and display departments located in 'Dharwad'.
